Rice & Ricotta Cake

8 servings

1	quart whole milk
¾	cup Arborio rice
1	teaspoon ground cinnamon
1	vanilla bean, split lengthwise
½	teaspoon coarse salt
1¼	cup granulated sugar
Unsalted butter for pan
All purpose flour for pan
3	pounds fresh ricotta drained 3 hours
3	large eggs plus 3 large yolks, lightly beaten
Confectioners sugar for dusting
Sauce – strawberry or raspberry

Preheat oven to 350F. Bring milk to a boil in large saucepan over medium-high heat. Stir in rice, cinnamon, salt, vanilla bean, and the seeds scraped from it. Reduce heat to medium-low; stirring occasionally with a wooden spoon, until rice is very tender and has absorbed all liquid, about 30 minutes. Remove pan from heat. Stir in ¾ cup sugar. Cover; let cool, stirring occasionally. Discard vanilla bean.
Butter and four a springform pan and set aside. In a large bowl, combine the rice mixture, ricotta, eggs, and remaining ½ cup of sugar. Pour into the prepared pan. Bake until golden on top and almost set in the center, 65 to 70 minutes; cover with foil if starting to brown too much. Transfer pan to a cooling rack.
When cake has completely cooled, run a knife around the edge to loosen. Gently remove the ring and transfer cake to a serving platter. Sprinkle with confectioners sugar and serve with a strawberry or raspberry sauce.
Berry Sauce: In a medium saucepan, combine 1 pint of strawberries or raspberries with 2 tablespoons of sugar and 2 teaspoons of freshly squeezed lemon juice. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally, until the berries are soft, 5 to 7 minutes. Puree berries in a food processor, then run through a sieve to remove all seeds.
