Garlic Flan with Portobello Mushrooms

4 servings

2	cups whipping cream
4	egg yolks
3	egg whites
1	garlic clove, smashed
1	large Portobello mushroom, cut into ½–inch slices
4	green onions, including tops, cut into 2-inch lengths
3	garlic cloves, sliced
2	tablespoons butter
3	teaspoons canola oil
¾	cup sherry
¾	cup chicken broth

For the Flan: Beat 3 egg yolks and 2 egg whites in a medium bowl. In a separate skillet, add the one smash clove of garlic to the cream and bring it just to a boil. Remove garlic, then add a  small bit of the milk to the eggs to bring them up in temperature. Add the rest of the milk to the eggs slowly while stirring, then add the final egg and stir. Add salt and pepper. Butter four ramekins, pour in flan mixture, set in water in a Bain-Marie, then bake in a 350F oven for 55-60 minutes until well set.
For the Mushroom Sauce: Heat butter and oil in a skillet for 2-3 minutes, then add the sliced Portobello mushroom and sauté for 2-3 minutes more. Add the green onions and garlic slices and sauté for 3-4 minutes more. Add the sherry and chicken broth and reduce by ¾.
For Presentation: Unmold garlic flans onto individual plates and serve with the mushroom and onion sauce.
