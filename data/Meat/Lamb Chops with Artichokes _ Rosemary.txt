Lamb Chops with Artichokes and Rosemary

4 servings

3	 tablespoons olive oil, divided
4	garlic cloves, minced, divided
4	teaspoons minced fresh rosemary, divided
4	teaspoons grated orange peel, divided
2	eight ounce packages of artichoke hearts, quartered
1½	cups chicken broth
8	one inch thick lamb chops, trimmed of most fat

Heat 2 tablespoons of the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add 2 teaspoons of the minced garlic cloves, 2 teaspoons of the chopped rosemary, and 2 teaspoons of the orange zest; stir for 30 seconds. Add the quartered artichoke hearts and chicken broth; bring to a boil. Cover and boil for 5 minutes, then uncover and simmer until sauce thickens, stirring occasionally, about 4 minutes. Remove from heat.
Salt and pepper both sides of the lamb chops, then sprinkle with the remaining garlic, rosemary, pressing into both sides. Heat remaining oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the lamb chops and sauté until cooked to desired doneness, about 4 minutes per side for medium-rare. Transfer lamb to platter or plates. Spoon fat from the lamb skillet, add artichoke mixture, boil 2 minutes, scraping up the browned bits. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Spoon artichoke mixture around the lamb chops, sprinkle with the remaining 2 teaspoons of orange zest, and serve.
