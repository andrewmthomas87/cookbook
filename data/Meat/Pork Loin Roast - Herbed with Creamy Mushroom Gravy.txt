Pork Loin Roast - Herbed with Creamy Mushroom Gravy

6 servings

1	teaspoon minced fresh rosemary leaves, or ¼ teaspoon dried rosemary
1	teaspoon minced fresh parsley leaves, or ¼ teaspoon parsley flakes
1	teaspoon minced fresh thyme leaves, or ¼ teaspoon dried thyme leaves
1	tablespoon minced garlic
2½	to 3 pounds boneless pork loin roast
2	cans (10¾-ounce) 98 percent fat-free cream of mushroom soup
1	cup skim milk
Brine
2	quarts of water
½	cup of salt
¼	cup of sugar

Preheat oven to 325F.
Brine the pork loin roast for 30 minutes at room temperature in 2 quarts of water with ½ cup of salt and ¼ cup of sugar.
Mix together rosemary, parsley, thyme and garlic. Cut small slits over surface of roast and insert herb mixture into slits. Place in a roasting pan. Roast until the internal temperature is 165F (about 1 hour). Remove roast from pan and let stand 10 minutes. Stir soup into pan drippings in roasting pan and heat over a medium flame. Gradually stir in skim milk. Continue to stir until mixture boils. Slice pork and serve with gravy.
