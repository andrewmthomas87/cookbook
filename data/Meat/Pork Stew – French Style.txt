Pork Stew – French Style

8-10 servings

6	parsley sprigs plus ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley
3	large sprigs fresh thyme
3	garlic cloves, unpeeled
2	bay leaves
1	tablespoon black peppercorns
2	whole cloves
5	cups water
3	pounds boneless pork butt roast trimmed & cut into 1 to 1½ inch pieces
1	meaty smoked ham shank
2	onions, halved & left intact
4	carrots, peeled & cut into ½ inch oueces
1	pound Yukon Gold potatoes, unpeeled & cut into ¾ inch pieces
12	ounces kielabasa sausage, halved lengthwise then cut into ½ inch slices
½	head savoy cabbage, shredded (8 cups)

Set oven rack to middle and preheat to 325F. Place parsley sprigs, thyme sprigs, garlic, bay leaves, peppercorns, andcloves in cheese cloth and tie into a bundle.
Bring water, broth, pork, ham, onions, and herb bundle to simmer in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat, skimming off scum. Cover pot and plce in oven. Cook until pork chunks are tender, 1¼ to 1½ hours.
Using a slotted spoon, di\scard onions and herb bundle. Transfer ham to a plate. Add carrots and potatoes to pot and stir to combine. Cover pot and return to oven. Cook until vegetables are tender, 20-25 minutes. Remove meat from hambone and shred into bite-size pieces.
Add shredded ham, kielbasa, and cabbage to pot. Stir to combine, cover, and cook another 15-20 minutes until cabbabe is wilted. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then stir in chopped parsley.  (Stew can be made up to 3 days in advance, then re-heated to serve.)
