Pork Tenderloin with Apples, Dates & Potatoes

4 servings

2	one-pound pork tenderloins, brined for 30 minutes
10	maple smoked bacon slices
1	tablespoons olive oil
2	small onions, peeled and quartered
2	Granny Smith apples, peeled, cored, and quartered
12	baby white-skinned potatoes, unpeeled
24	Medjool dates, pitted
4	garlic cloves, minced
1	teaspoon ground cinnamon
1	teaspoon ground cumin
¾	cup apple juice
½	cup water

Brine the pork tenderloins for 30 minutes in ¼ cup of salt in 2 quarts of water. Remove and dry, then rub with olive oil and season with salt, pepper.
Wrap each with pork tenderloin with bacon, securing with toothpicks. Tie kitchen string around each bacon-wrapped tenderloin to secure, then remove the toothpicks.
Preheat oven to 350F. Heat oil in a large ovenproof pot over medium heat. Add the pork tenderloins and cook until browned on both sides, turning often, about 12 minutes. Transfer to a platter. Add onions and apples to the pot; sauté until golden, about 5 minutes. Add potatoes, dates, garlic, cinnamon, and cumin; stir for 1 minte. Return pork tenderloins to the center of the pot. Add the apple juice and ¼ cup of water and bring to a boil, scrpaing up any browned bits. Sprinkle with salt and pepper. Cover and roast until pork is cooked through and the potatoes are tender, about 30 minutes.
Remove the strings from the pork tenderloins and slice crosswise. Divide the pork onions, apples, potatoes, dates, and pan juices among plates and serve.
