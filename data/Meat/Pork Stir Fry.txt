Pork Stir Fry

4 servings

1	pound lean boneless pork
⅔	cup soy sauce
¼	cup brown sugar
1	tablespoon cornstarch
½	teaspoon red pepper flakes
1	red bell pepper cut into strips
4	scallions, chopped
2	cloves garlic, minced
1	tablespoon cooking oil
1	tablespoon chicken broth or water
1	tablespoon toasted sesame seed
2	teaspoons sesame oil
2	teaspoons fresh ginger, minced
1	can bamboo shoots
Green vegetables (broccoli, snow peas, or asparagus)

Whisk together soy sauce, brown sugar and cornstarch. Add ginger, garlic and red pepper flakes. Cut chops into thin strips and add to soy sauce mixture. Toss to coat, cover and refrigerate for at least 15 minutes.
Heat 1 teaspoon of the sesame oil in large skillet or wok, over high heat. Remove pork from marinade & add to skillet, reserving marinade. Stir fry 1-2 minutes or until no longer pink. Remove from skillet with slotted spoon & keep warm.
Add remaining sesame oil to skillet. Add bell pepper, snow peas and green onions. Stir fry 2-3 minutes. Return pork to skillet & stir in reserved marinade and bamboo shoots. Bring to a boil & cook 1 minute or until sauce has thickened.
