Pork Paprikas

4 servings

4	pork loin pieces, cut into serving size
2	tablespoons butter
½	cup onion, chopped
2	large garlic cloves, chopped
2	tablespoons sweet Hungarian paprika
1	teaspoon salt
2	large Italian plum tomatoes, chopped
1	large green bell pepper, chopped
¾	cup chicken broth
¼	cup flour
¼	cup light cream
½	cup sour cream

In a heavy kettle, heat the butter, add the onions and garlic and saute until lighly browned. Add the paprika, salt, tomato, green pepper, and stock. Cover and cook for 10 minutes.
Add the pork tenderloin pieces, cover and cook until tender, about 40 minutes. Add water or additional chicken stock, if necessary, to make one and one-quarter cups of broth.
Blend the flour with the light cream and a little of the hot sauce until it makes a smooth slurry; add it to the kettle and cook, stirring, until thickened.
Stir in the sour cream and simmer until heated through. Do not let boil. Serve over noodles or rice.
