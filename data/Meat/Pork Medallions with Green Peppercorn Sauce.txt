Pork Medallions with Green Peppercorn Sauce

4 servings

1½	pounds pork tenderloin cut into 1-inch thick slices
2	teaspoons Dijon mustard
1	tablespoons drained green peppercorns
½	cup dry vermouth (or dry white wine)
3	tablespoons butter
⅓ cup whipping cream
1½	teaspoon minced fresh or ½ teaspoon dried, crumbled thyme
1½	cups chicken stock or canned low-salt chicken broth
3	shallots, minced
Fresh thyme or parsley leaves for garnish

Flatten pork slights with palm of hand or mallet.  Sprinkle with salt and pepper. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in heavy large skillet over medium-high heat. Add pork and cook until brown, about 3 minutes per side.  Transfer to plate.
Melt remaining 1-tablespoon butter in same skillet. Add shallots and sauté 2 minutes. Add vermouth and boil until reduced to glaze, about 5 minutes. Add stock, peppercorns and minced thyme and boil until reduced to ¼ cup, about 10 minutes. Whisk in mustard, then cream.  Season with salt and pepper.
Return pork and collected juices on the plate to the skillet.  Simmer until pork is cooked and sauce thickens slightly, about 3 minutes. Sprinkle with parsley or thyme for garnish.
